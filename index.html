Human & Molecular Genetics · Genomics · Transcriptomics · Bioinformatics

My research interests span human and molecular genetics, genomics, transcriptomics, and the genetic and molecular basis of human disease, with particular interests in rare disease genomics, neurogenetics, RNA biology, epigenetics, and functional genomics. My background combines molecular genetics with experience in Sanger sequence analysis, WES variant analysis, bulk RNA-seq, TCGA-based validation, R, and Linux-based bioinformatics workflows. I have also completed comprehensive training in single-cell RNA-seq and continue to expand my skills in genomic and transcriptomic analysis.

Selected Projects

IPAH Transcriptomics

Bulk RNA-seq analysis of publicly available IPAH and control datasets, including sample and metadata curation, count-matrix preparation, batch-adjusted differential expression analysis using DESeq2, sex-stratified analysis, and prioritisation of candidate genes.

TNBC Single-Cell Transcriptomics

Collaborative single-cell transcriptomics project in triple-negative breast cancer. My contribution focused on TCGA-based validation and downstream analysis of candidate genes identified from the single-cell study.

Rare Disease Variant Analysis

Case-based training in whole-exome sequencing for Mendelian disorders, including variant filtering, annotation, prioritisation and clinical interpretation.
